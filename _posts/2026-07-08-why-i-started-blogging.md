In an age of short-form content and algorithm-driven feeds, starting a personal blog feels almost like an act of rebellion. Here's why I decided to do it anyway.

## Owning My Content

Social media platforms come and go. Twitter might implode, TikTok might get banned, but my blog — hosted on my own domain, backed by simple HTML files — will outlast them all. When you publish on someone else's platform, you're renting space. When you blog, you own the land.

## Writing to Think

There's a famous quote: "I write to know what I think." Blogging forces clarity. When you have to explain something to an audience, even an imaginary one, you can't hide behind hand-wavy reasoning. The gaps in your understanding become painfully obvious — and that's a good thing.

## Building in Public

Sharing my projects, my learning process, and even my failures creates a body of work that speaks louder than any resume. It's a portfolio of thought that evolves over time.

## A Quiet Corner of the Internet

Not everything needs to go viral. Some ideas deserve a quiet space to breathe. This blog is that space — for me, and hopefully for the few readers who stumble upon it.

If you've been thinking about starting a blog, consider this your sign. The internet could use more personal, thoughtful writing.
